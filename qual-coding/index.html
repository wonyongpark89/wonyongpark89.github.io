Definition — Category

A category is composed of codes that "look alike and feel alike." Categories are formed by grouping codes into patterns. Like a code, a category is a word or short phrase — but more condensed and operating at a higher level of abstraction.

Look across all your codes and drag those that look and feel alike into a category. Name each one and describe what holds them together. Not every code needs to go into a category — leftover codes are fine.

Definition — Theme

A theme is an extended phrase or sentence that identifies what a unit of data is about and/or what it means. Compared with categories, themes are more interpretive, more descriptive, and often more subtle.

The analytic goals here are to winnow down the number of themes to explore, and to develop either an overarching theme from the data corpus, or an integrative theme that weaves various themes together into a coherent narrative. What is going on here? What does this tell us?
